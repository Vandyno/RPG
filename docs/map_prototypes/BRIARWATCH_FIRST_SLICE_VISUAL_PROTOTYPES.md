# Briarwatch First-Slice Visual Prototype Handoff

Status: non-canon visual prototype only.

These mockups test art and layout direction for a possible small first playable
area near Briarwatch, on the northwest edge of the Marches of Velcor near the
Elderweald / Greenwood Boundary.

They do not place final POIs. They are not a playable map. They are not
worldgen. They are not canon unless approved.

## Chosen Area

Working area: Briarwatch frontier road edge.

Atlas translation:
- The continent map shows Briarwatch as a frontier town near the Elderweald.
- For playable space, this becomes one local road leaving human settlement
  control and pressing toward managed forest / old-growth boundary.
- The first-slice feel should be close, readable, and physical: road, fence,
  timber signs of human work, then darker forest pressure.

Core visual problem:
- Make the player feel they are leaving human Marches safety and approaching
  contested Rootborn-adjacent woodland without needing a large map.

## Mockup A: Settlement Edge

Image:
- `docs/map_prototypes/briarwatch_edge_concept_a_settlement_edge.png`

Tests:
- How a small Briarwatch edge could read on screen.
- Dirt road scale versus player scale.
- Settlement edge without locking final building layout.
- Managed timber, fences, low walls, and replanted trees.
- Clear transition from human-maintained space to darker woods.

Useful direction:
- Good readable road shape.
- Strong player scale.
- Nice contrast between fenced work yard and forest edge.

Risk:
- Looks close to a concrete settlement layout. Treat buildings as mood only,
  not final structures or POIs.

Tests:

Useful direction:

## Mockup C: Brook Crossing

Image:
- `docs/map_prototypes/briarwatch_edge_concept_c_brook_crossing.png`

Tests:
- Water edge style for a small local brook, not River Veyn.
- Mud, reeds, roots, ford stones, and plank crossing.
- Whether a road crossing can stay readable for movement.

  needs it.

## Shared Art Direction Notes

Terrain:
- Painterly, grounded, dark storybook.
- Tile-aware readability without visible grid lines.
- Roads should be obvious walkable routes.
- Grass and mud should support movement readability, not become visual noise.

Roads / paths:
- Roads should curve naturally.
- Main road can be cart-wide.
- Forest paths can narrow to imply risk and lower authority.

Tree density:
- Human-managed land: sparse, younger trees, stumps, timber piles.
- Boundary land: mixed density, more brush, stones, and roots.
- Old forest: dense canopy, larger trunks, darker green, heavier shadow.

Settlement / wilderness edge:
- Settlement should be implied by fence, road work, timber, and a few structures.
- Wilderness should feel close and old, not empty.
- Avoid final named landmarks until content is approved.

Player scale:
- Player should stay small against trees, roads, fences, and water.
- Environment should feel larger than the character, but not unreadable.

## Recommendation

Use Mockup B as the strongest direction for the first visual pass.

Blend in:
- Mockup A for settlement-edge language.
- Mockup C only if the first slice benefits from a small crossing.

Do not implement from these images directly. Use them as visual direction for
future tile art, terrain palette, and playable-space composition.
