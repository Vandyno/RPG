- town placement

# Preferred Style

Target style:
- readable fantasy atlas
- modern overhead terrain feel
- clear labels
- visible roads and borders
- practical geography
- less parchment clutter
- region names that can actually be read

Clarity and usability come first.

# Map Reading

## Regional Layout

The Marches of Velcor form the central human heartland. Cairnwall anchors the
western-central side of the heartland, while Dunmere anchors the river trade
network near River Veyn.

The Elderweald dominates the northwest. Mosshollow sits inside it, and
Blackrook Archive lies near the northwest coast.

The Shadegroves sit north of the human heartland and east of the Elderweald.
The Greenwood Boundary separates human-managed lands from Rootborn-protected
territory.

The Tangle Heights occupy the northeast, with Stepcliff Monastery high in the
mountains and Branchrest on the southern approach toward Lake Orin.

The Iron March occupies the southeast beyond the Greyfang Range and the Silent
Barrows. Karag-Tor and Ashforge Hold sit along the Oathroad.

The Mireveil fills the southwest coast and wetlands below Redfield and Oakholt.
The Weeping Vale sits south of the Red Fields and west of the Greyfang Range.

The Blackwater lies east of Lake Orin and feels remote, dark, and forbidding.

## Major Roads And Travel

The heartland road network is dense and practical.

Important road logic:
- Cairnwall, Dunmere, and Hallowrest form a major human triangle.
- Briarwatch sits on the northwest frontier road toward the Elderweald.
- Northgate and Stonebridge sit near the northern routes and river crossing.
- Greenford links the central heartland toward Lake Orin and Branchrest.
- Dunmere is the most important river and trade hub.
- Fairmead and Mirthbridge sit on southern/eastern trade roads near Dunmere.
- The Oathroad runs through the southeast Iron March, linking Karag-Tor and
  Ashforge Hold.
- Southwatch is isolated near the southern edge of the Mireveil and Weeping
  Vale.

## Rivers And Water

River Veyn is the central river route. It passes through or near Dunmere and
connects northern water flow to the southern heartland.

Lake Orin is east of Dunmere and Greenford. It has at least two visible nearby
settlements:
- Laford
- Duskford

The Blackwater lies beyond Lake Orin to the east.

## Cult Influence Markers

The map legend marks three cult influence types:
- Ashen Choir
- Noonday Cult
- Hollow Mercy

Visible influence markers include:
- Hollow Mercy or related purple markers near Redfield/Oakholt, Dunmere, the
  road east of Greenford, and the Silent Barrows.
- Ashen Choir markers near Karag-Tor and Ashforge Hold.
- A Noonday Cult marker appears in or near Dunmere.

These markers should be treated as pressure points for quest generation, not
automatically as fully public cult control.

# Map Generation Prompt

Create a clean, readable fantasy world map for a dark storybook RPG setting.
Use a realistic overhead atlas style with clear terrain, readable labels, and a
polished professional RPG map presentation. The map should feel closer to a
Google Earth or atlas-style fantasy map than a decorative parchment
illustration.

Show realistic terrain placement: dark green forests, pale farmland and plains,
gray rocky mountains, blue rivers and lakes, brown highlands, dark swamp,
rugged coast, roads, political boundaries, cultural boundaries, settlement
icons, and region labels.

Preserve these major named places and keep them clearly labeled:

The Marches of Velcor, Cairnwall, Dunmere, Hallowrest, Briarwatch, The
Elderweald, The Shadegroves, Mosshollow, The Greenwood Boundary, The Tangle
Heights, Stepcliff Monastery, Branchrest, Blackrook Archive, The Last Perch,
Lake Orin, The Blackwater, The Greyfang Range, The Iron March, Karag-Tor,
Ashforge Hold, The Oathroad, The Mireveil, The Weeping Vale, The Silent
Barrows, The Red Fields, Hollow Coast, and River Veyn.

Make settlements, roads, borders, and region boundaries easy to distinguish.
Include a small clean legend, north arrow, and scale bar, but keep them
unobtrusive.
